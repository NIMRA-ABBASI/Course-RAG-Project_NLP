Shortly afterwards, the two captives were released and blood money was given to 
the killed man’s father.[For details see Za'd Al-Ma'ad 2/83-85; Ibn Hisham 1/605; Rahmat-ul-lil'alameen 1/115, 2/468] 
After this event, Quraish began to realize the real danger that Madinah could present 
with. They came to know that Madinah had always been on the alert, watching 
closely their commercial caravans. It was then common knowledge to them that the 
Muslims in their new abode could span and extend their military activities over an 
area of 300 miles. and bring it under full control. However, the new situation borne 
in mind, the Makkans could not be deterred and were too obstinate to come to terms 
with the new rising power of Islam. They were determined to bring their fall by their 
own hands and with this recklessness they precipitated the great battle of Badr. 
The Muslims, on the other hand, and at the behest of their Lord, were ordered to go 
to war in Sha‘ban 2 A.H:
